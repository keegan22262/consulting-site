We are refining the Insight Cards used on the Insights & Perspectives page.

The current implementation works structurally but requires several important improvements.

The goal is to elevate the cards to an editorial, cinematic design similar to the insight cards used by leading consulting firms.

---

IMAGE UNIQUENESS

Each insight must have its own unique image.

No two insights should share the same image.

Images must visually represent the theme of the insight.

Examples:

Mobile connectivity insight → telecom infrastructure / mobile networks
Financial inclusion insight → digital banking or mobile payments
Supply-chain resilience → ports, logistics hubs, container terminals
Cybersecurity → digital security operations center
AI governance → AI control rooms or algorithmic systems
Public sector digital transformation → digital government operations

Images must be high-resolution and cinematic.

Avoid generic stock imagery.

---

CINEMATIC IMAGE STYLE

Images must create an atmospheric feeling.

They should visually communicate the insight topic even before the user reads the headline.

Recommended visual style:

• infrastructure megaprojects
• technology command centers
• financial trading environments
• government operations rooms
• supply-chain logistics hubs
• energy systems and power infrastructure

Images should feel calm, cinematic, and institutional.

---

CARD SIZE

The insight cards are currently too small.

Increase the overall card size significantly.

Cards should feel editorial and immersive.

Recommended layout:

Desktop grid:
3 cards per row

Each card should be taller than the current design.

Increase the image height so the image becomes the dominant visual element.

---

TEXT OVER IMAGE

Move the insight text onto the image instead of below the image.

The image must act as the background.

Apply a navy gradient overlay on the image to maintain text readability.

Text layout:

Category label (top-left)

Insight headline (large and dominant)

Short description

Metadata line:

RSL Perspectives • Month Year • Reading time

CTA link:

Read Insight →

The text should sit toward the lower-left portion of the card.

---

CARD CORNER STYLE

The current cards have corners that are too sharp.

Apply the same rounded-card style used across the homepage.

Cards should have soft rounded corners.

The visual tone should feel modern and calm.

---

HOVER INTERACTION

When the cursor moves over the card:

• the image subtly darkens
• the headline becomes slightly larger
• the CTA "Read Insight →" becomes more visible
• the card lifts slightly with a soft shadow

Animations must be subtle and smooth.

Avoid aggressive transitions.

---

CONSISTENCY

The insight cards must remain fully clickable.

Clicking the card must open the corresponding detailed insight page using the existing link structure.

Do not change the insight links.

Preserve all existing URLs.

---

FINAL RESULT

The insight cards should feel like editorial research pieces rather than blog posts.

Visitors should immediately feel that the insights are part of an institutional research platform.

The design must remain scalable to support 50+ insights in the future.
